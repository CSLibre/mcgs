What's New in 2026

Eight major themes introduced or substantially expanded in the 2026 revision

🤖

AI Literacy (central theme)

AI tools, limitations, and ethics woven across every strand and stage — not just one Stage 9 objective as in 2019.

✍️

Prompt Engineering

Stages 7–9 now explicitly teach prompt creation, prompting frameworks (RTF, CORE), iterative refinement, and source triangulation.

🧠

"Main Creator" Principle

Students must retain creative and intellectual ownership when using AI. Multiple objectives across all stages reinforce this.

💬

Affective & Agentic AI

Entirely new concepts: Affective AI (emotion detection), Agentic AI (autonomous action), AGI and ASI predictions.

🌍

AI & Climate / Energy

Data centres' energy use, AI's carbon footprint, and the environmental dimension of digital divides are now curriculum content.

🔒

Deeper Cybersecurity

Phishing, pharming, social engineering, hacking, fraud — treated as crimes with real human victims, not just abstract risks.

⚖️

AI Regulation & Ethics

Industry regulation, moral rights of creators, legal variation across jurisdictions, and ethical implications of agentic systems.

❤️

Anthropomorphic Risk

Stage 9 now addresses emotional, social and security risks of treating AI chatbots as human relationships.
